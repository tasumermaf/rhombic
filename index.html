The Thesis

Two lattice topologies. Same number of nodes. Radically different structure.

Cubic — 6 neighbors

The Cartesian default. Each cell touches 6 face-sharing neighbors. Linear memory, square pixels, cubic voxels. The spatial expression of orthogonal coordinates.

Every node must route through 6 edges. Removing one edge cuts 17% of connections. A single failure creates a bottleneck.

# The default grid[x][y][z].neighbors = 6 diameter ~ 3(n^1/3 - 1)

FCC — 12 neighbors

The densest sphere packing (Kepler, proved Hales 2005). Voronoi cells are rhombic dodecahedra. 12 faces, 12 connections. The lattice nature uses for copper, aluminum, gold.

12 edges per node means 12 alternative routes. Removing one edge costs 8% of connections. The network routes around failures.

# The alternative fcc[i].neighbors = 12 diameter ~ 2(n^1/3 - 1) fiedler ~ 2.4x cubic
